Understanding Sleep Cycles

N1 Stage (Dozing Off)

  • 1-7 minutes duration
  • Light sleep, easy to wake
  • Body starts relaxing
  • Brain waves slow down
  • May experience hypnic jerks

N2 Stage (Light Sleep)

  • 50% of total sleep time
  • Heart rate decreases
  • Body temperature drops
  • Memory consolidation
  • Less awareness of surroundings

N3 Stage (Deep Sleep)

  • Starts ~40 minutes after falling asleep
  • Critical for restoration
  • Hormone regulation
  • Tissue repair
  • Immune system boost

REM Stage (Dream Sleep)

  • Lightest sleep stage
  • Active brain activity
  • Memory and mood regulation
  • Dreaming occurs
  • Duration increases with each cycle

Effects of Sleep Deprivation

Short-term Effects

  • Fatigue and irritability
  • Trouble concentrating
  • Slower reaction times
  • Impaired judgment
  • Daytime sleepiness
  • Mood swings
  • Memory issues

Long-term Effects

  • Higher risk of obesity
  • Heart disease and stroke
  • Diabetes
  • Cognitive problems
  • Memory loss
  • Anxiety and depression
  • Increased dementia risk

What Happens During Sleep

Body Temperature

Drops 1-2 degrees to conserve energy

Heart Rate

Slows during non-REM, increases during REM

Metabolism

Decreases, affecting hunger hormones

Brain Activity

Processes emotions and consolidates memories
